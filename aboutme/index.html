About Me

A little bit of my story

I was born in 1997, in a small village called Vitoria (which means victory) in a small state of Brazil. Life was not luxurus, in fact my parents always fought a lot for me, and for that I'll always be grateful. At age of four, I moved to Portugal. Life in Brazil was complicated, so we decided to move, which I later on in my life discovered that would be somthing that I would be doing quite a while. Our life in Portugal was good, we always had what to eat, we made new friends and my parents found jobs that were good for them. I loved my school, and as the time went by I learned the real meaning of being an immigrant, but I dind't feel as an outsider, Portugal was a plce that I could call home. But at the age of nineteen, I moved to France with my parents. I thought that moving to Portugal had beem the biggest challenge of my life, but I was mistaken, for sure moving to France changed completely my thoughts on that. Moving to a country where you don't speak the language can be a little bit tricky. You feel frustrated and usuless at first, you feel that you will never learn that language, but with time and patience things slowly get better but after two years in France, I hadn't quite yet had the feeling of bellowing there.

I always had the desire to move to England, so I did. I made the decision to study in the UK. Even with all my insecurities and doubts, I came here alone to follow my dreams. And now I'm studying Media, Journalism and Publishing at Oxford Brookes University, which I never thought I would be possible.

My favourite book

To Kill a Mokingbird

To Kill a Mockingbird is a novel by Harper Lee. It was written in 1960 but it is set in the mid-1930s in the small town of Moycomb, Alabama. It is narrated by Scout Finch, a six-year-old tomboy who lives with her father Atticus.´and her ten-year-old brother Jem. During the novel Atticus is asked to defend Tom Robinson, a black man wrongly accused of raping a white woman. Atticus takes on the case even though everyone knows he has little hope of winning. As readers we learn some valuable life lessons, tolerance and empathy.
